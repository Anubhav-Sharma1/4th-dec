About WHO

The World Health Organization (WHO) is a specialized agency of the United Nations responsible for international public health. It was established in 1948 and is headquartered in Geneva, Switzerland. WHO's mission is to promote and protect global health by setting standards and providing guidance on health matters worldwide.

Did You Know?

The WHO was instrumental in the global response to the COVID-19 😷 pandemic. They provide guidance, resources, and expert advice to governments and healthcare organizations to mitigate the impact of the virus.

WHO also addresses other global health issues, such as nutrition, mental health, infectious diseases, and more.
